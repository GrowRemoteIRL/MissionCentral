🤝 Building Community: Your Checklist

You don't need a big plan to begin. Your local impact starts with one small step. Pick a practical action and get going. Momentum comes quickly once you start.

Pick your event type

After-work drinks, coffee morning, pizza & chats, walk & talk, or cowork day. Start with what feels natural.

Choose a venue (or don't worry yet)

A pub, cafe, park, or hub works. Don't let this slow you down. You can decide the week of.

Set up your Eventbrite listing

Use the Grow Remote Eventbrite organiser. Victoria or the community team can help you get set up.

View events on Eventbrite →

Promote in your area

Share in your regional WhatsApp group, post on local social media, tell the hub or coworking space near you.

Run the event

Show up, welcome people, keep it casual. No presentations needed. The goal is connection.

Fill in the post-event form

Upload photos, share numbers and stories. This feeds our impact data and helps secure funding.

Claim your €100 event funding

Grow Remote funds up to €100 per community event. Submit receipts after your event.

🛤️ Remote Work Pathways: Your Checklist

Making remote work visible changes lives. When people in your area see that remote work is real and accessible, they start looking for it. You become the bridge between opportunity and community.

Attend or organise a careers fair

Bring Grow Remote materials to local careers fairs, school events, or jobs expos. We'll send you a resource pack.

Give a talk at a school or college

Share your remote work story with students. Help them see that they don't have to leave their area for a good career.

Connect with local employers

Know a company that hires remotely? Introduce them to Grow Remote. Every employer connection strengthens the network.

Share remote job opportunities

When you spot a remote job that could work for someone in your area, share it in the community.

View career resources →

Promote our training programmes

Lead from Anywhere and Futureproof Your Career are fully funded and built from 9 years of IP with leading employers.

🏘️ Community Partnerships: Your Checklist

You don't need to do it alone. Partnering with hubs, charities, and local groups multiplies your reach. Some of the best Grow Remote events happen because someone picked up the phone and said "let's do something together."

Connect with your local hub

Remote working hubs, enterprise centres, and libraries are natural partners. Many will host your events for free.

Partner with a local charity

Volunteering events build community and visibility. Cork Simon, ALONE, Irish Cancer Society, and Daffodil Day are all great fits.

Reach out to your local council

Local authorities are interested in remote work as a tool for regional development. Introduce them to Grow Remote's work.

Co-host with another chapter

Cross-county events bring fresh energy. Connect with leaders in neighbouring areas through the WhatsApp group.

Organise a community clean-up or charity event

Remote workers giving back locally. It shows the community that remote work brings people closer, not further apart.

🌎 The mission 📈 Key stat 💼 Remote jobs
Remote work isn't a trend. It's a permanent shift in where work happens. But there's no national plan to win remote jobs into Ireland. No target. No agency. Meanwhile, over 100,000 remote roles are advertised across Europe every single month. Grow Remote is building the case for Ireland's first national target for remote job creation. You can help in 2 minutes: https://growremote.ie/mission-central/ #RemoteWork #GrowRemote #Ireland
